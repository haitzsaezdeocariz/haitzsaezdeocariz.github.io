Ocáriz

Haitz Sáez de Ocáriz Borde is a PhD researcher at the University of Oxford. Ocáriz is a member of Christ Church College. His research focuses on Geometric Deep Learning and Deep Generative Models. He is interested in theoretical machine learning as well as applications to robotics and rocket science. Prior to joining the University of Oxford, Ocáriz completed an MPhil in Machine Learning and Machine Intelligence at the University of Cambridge, and an MEng in Aerospace Engineering at Imperial College London with a placement at Harvard University. Following the completion of his MEng, Ocáriz also worked as a researcher at Harvard University and other prestigious institutions.

Geometric Deep Learning

Geometric deep learning refers to a subfield of machine learning that aims to develop neural network architectures capable of processing and analyzing structured data with a geometric or topological nature, such as graphs and manifolds. My particular focus lies in the analysis of the geometry of latent spaces in machine learning algorithms.

Generative Models

Generative machine learning models have the ability to generate new data samples that are similar to their training data, which could potentially enable them to create artwork that resembles human creations. As this technology advances, it has the potential to eventually generate a vast range of content, limited only by human imagination.

Rocket Science

We are currently developing computationally inexpensive AI-based aerothermodynamic simulator models of rockets and reentry vehicles at realistic flight conditions with applications to aerodynamic design and mission analysis and control.
